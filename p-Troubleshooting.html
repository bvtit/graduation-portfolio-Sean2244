Troubleshooting Network Connectivity

  1. Home
  2. Portfolio Item

Description

First, my class was tasked with completing a complicated troubleshooting lab. My lab group and I were able to finish the lab in a few hours; however, some of the class was having trouble finding a solution. I was tasked by my instructor to help find a solution. Unfortunately, while trying to find a solution, several more problems were created by the group. Instead of deleting all of the configurations, I had to analyze the several ip addresses, static routes, and basic configuations of all of the network devices for both IPv4 and IPv6. In addition, I had to look for errors in the physical topology. After extensive testing and analysis, I was able to solve all of the issues that were preventing network connectivity. Some of the errors were in correctly configured ip addresses, static and default routes, and errors in the physical topology. Overall, this allowed me to build on my troubleshooting skills and improve my knowledge of internetworking.

Details

  • Lab was from chapter 2 of CCNA Routing and Switching
  • Lab required three routers, two switches, and threee PCs
