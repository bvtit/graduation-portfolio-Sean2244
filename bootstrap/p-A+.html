Description

Getting my A+ certification has been my favorite achievement of my IT career thus far. Getting to the certification was an extensive process that spanned over freshman year to the beginning of junior year. I first started learning the curriculum for the A+ exam through the book, A+ Guide to IT Technical Support. I also learned from the online program, TestOut PCPro. The CompTIA A+ certification requires one to pass two different exams: the comptia 220-901, which is primarily based on hardware, and 220-902, which is primarily based on troubleshooting and software (it has now adapted to 1001 and 1002). I passed the 220-901 at the end of my sophomore year and the 220-902 at the beginning of my junior year. Most importantly, this certfication helped prove my knowledge in the IT field.
